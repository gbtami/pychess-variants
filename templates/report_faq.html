Report FAQ

What are reports for?

Reports help moderators keep fair play and respectful communication standards. If you believe a user has broken the Terms of Service, submit a report so the incident can be reviewed.

Why does PyChess need user reports?

Community reports are an important signal for moderation. They help staff find incidents faster, especially when context is spread across games, profiles, and private messages.

What should I include in a report?

Provide concrete context: links to games, tournaments, or profiles, plus a short explanation of what happened. Specific evidence allows moderators to verify the issue quickly.

What happens after I send a report?

Reports go to the moderation queue. A moderator reviews the details and can issue warnings, communication restrictions, or stronger actions for serious or repeated abuse. Review can take some time depending on queue volume.

Will I be informed about the result?

You may receive a notification for some outcomes, but not every action is announced to reporters. No follow-up message does not mean the report was ignored.

Why can a reported account still be active?

Some actions are limited in scope. For example, communication-related sanctions can restrict chat/community participation without fully blocking game play.
