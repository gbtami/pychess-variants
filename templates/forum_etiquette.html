Forum Etiquette

The forum is for useful discussion between players. Moderators may close or remove threads that break site rules. Following these guidelines keeps conversations readable and productive.

A descriptive title

Pick a clear subject line so people can understand your thread before opening it.

Good: "Tournament feature request: from-position pairings"

Bad: "Help", "Bug", "Question", "Hi"

Advertising and spam

Promotional posts are not allowed in forum threads.

  • Do not post "follow me", "join my team", "join my tournament", or channel promotion threads.
  • Do not create engagement bait threads such as "how many likes can this get".
  • If you see spam, use the report button on that post instead of replying to it.

Use the correct sub-forum

Choose the category that matches your topic.

  • General Chess Discussion for broad chess and variant discussion.
  • PyChess Feedback for bug reports and feature feedback.
  • Game Analysis for sharing and discussing games.
  • Off-Topic Discussion for non-chess conversation.

Respect other players

  • Explain disagreement calmly and focus on ideas, not people.
  • No insults, slurs, harassment, or profanity directed at other users.
  • Do not escalate arguments into personal fights.
  • If a post is abusive or trolling, report it instead of retaliating.

No public shaming

Do not make accusation threads about named users ("is X cheating?", "X is toxic", etc.). Use the dedicated report form instead: /report.

Before posting

  • Search the forum first to avoid duplicate topics.
  • Read existing FAQ or docs for common questions.
  • Keep posts on-topic and readable.
